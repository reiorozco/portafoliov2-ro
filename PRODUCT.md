# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and tech leads evaluating Rei Orozco for **remote Full Stack + AI roles** (US and LATAM markets); secondarily, potential freelance clients. Context of use: a fast, often-skeptical scan — frequently on mobile, low patience — usually cross-referencing the site against Rei's CV, LinkedIn, and GitHub. The job to be done: decide in under a minute whether Rei is a credible senior engineer worth a reply.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio whose goal is to land remote Full Stack + AI work. The design *is* the product: a visitor's impression is the deliverable. Success = a recruiter clicks through to a live project or the contact form, and the perception "senior, full-stack, AI-capable" lands without Rei having to argue it.

## Brand Personality

**Clean · minimal · refined.** Precise, confident, senior — never junior, never salesy. Dark, technical, intentional. The reference feel is Linear / Vercel: restraint as confidence, strong typography, micro-detail over decoration. The interactive 3D hero is the one permitted flourish; it carries the personality so the rest of the page can stay quiet.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate-boring / soulless.** Stock, safe, invisible. Zero risk reads as zero personality.
- **Overloaded / noisy.** Too many effects, colors, or animations competing for attention.
- **Generic portfolio template.** The recognizable "AI/template" look — repeated emoji kickers above every heading, rows of vanity metrics, generic "Clean Code / Team Player" filler cards, rounded icons over every title. (Actively being removed.)

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Proof through live projects, real company names, and links — not adjectives about being "passionate" or "hard-working."
2. **Senior restraint.** Confidence comes from reduction. If an element is there to fill space or impress, cut it.
3. **Every element earns its place.** No scaffolding, no filler, no repeated section grammar. One intentional flourish (the 3D), everything else supports it.
4. **Coherence across surfaces.** Portfolio, CV, LinkedIn, and GitHub must tell the same story (same companies, same flagships, same positioning).
5. **Distinctive, not loud.** Stand out by being precise and intentional, not by adding noise. The opposite of both anti-references.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Maintain skip-to-content link, semantic landmarks, one `<h1>`, descriptive `alt` text, visible focus states, and body contrast ≥4.5:1. Known gap to watch: heavy GSAP/Three.js motion has **no `prefers-reduced-motion` path yet** — entrance animations and the 3D scene should degrade for motion-sensitive users.
